PACE MISSION FIRST SPRING BLOOM

Snow is still melting in the Northeastern United States and Canada, creating a sediment plume from snowmelt runoff in the St Lawrence River . Meanwhile, the Northwestern Atlantic is blooming! The region, including the Georges Bank and the Gulf of Maine , is known for its high biological productivity, which made it a historical commercial fisheries hotspot. However, overfishing and climate change are threatening the ecosystem.

This image was captured by the OCI instrument aboard PACE on April 8, 2024.

North Sea Hues

North Sea Hues

Spring phytoplankton blooms color the North Sea, lying between Norway, Great Britain, and Northern Europe. The North Sea is a very diverse environment, with marked variations in depth, temperature, and salinity, which influences the distribution of the fauna residing in the basin. The ecosystem is unfortunately affected by human activity in this densely populated area, where overfishing, dredging and pollution are some of the factors that contribute to its degradation.

This image was captured by the OCI instrument aboard PACE on May 15, 2024.
